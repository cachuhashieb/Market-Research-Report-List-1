<p><p>Microcrystalline Cellulose (MCC) is a purified, partially depolymerized cellulose used widely as an excipient in pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics. The MCC market is segmented into two main types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based. Wood pulp-based MCC is derived from natural wood sources, offering a cost-effective option with high availability. In contrast, refined cotton-based MCC is sourced from cotton fibers, providing superior purity and a finer texture, often preferred in high-end applications requiring stringent quality standards.</p></p>
