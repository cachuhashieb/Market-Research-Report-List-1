<p><p>The cat DNA test market comprises two main types: swab tests and blood tests. Swab tests involve collecting DNA samples via buccal swabs from the cat's cheek, offering a non-invasive and user-friendly option for pet owners. Blood tests, on the other hand, require a blood sample, providing potentially more comprehensive genetic information but necessitating veterinary assistance. Both methods serve to identify breed, health traits, and potential genetic disorders, catering to the growing demand for personalized pet care and lineage tracking.</p></p>
