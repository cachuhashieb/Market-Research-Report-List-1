<p><p>The flame-retardant thermoplastic market is categorized into two main types: amorphous polymers and crystalline polymers. Amorphous polymers, such as polycarbonate and polystyrene, offer excellent impact resistance and flexibility, making them suitable for applications requiring high strength and versatility. In contrast, crystalline polymers, like polyethylene and polypropylene, show better thermal stability and chemical resistance but may have limited flexibility. Both types are essential in various industries, including electronics, automotive, and construction, where fire safety is a critical concern.</p></p>
