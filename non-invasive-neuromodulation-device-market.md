<p><p>The non-invasive neuromodulation device market encompasses various types, including Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS), which delivers a constant electrical current to modulate neural activity. Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS) uses alternating currents to influence brain rhythms. Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS) focuses on delivering pulses to enhance specific brain functions, while Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS) applies random noise for cognitive enhancements. Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT), though more invasive, remains relevant for severe mental health conditions. Other devices in the market address various neurological and psychiatric disorders.</p></p>
