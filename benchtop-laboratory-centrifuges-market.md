<p><p>Benchtop laboratory centrifuges are categorized into two primary types: high-speed and low-speed centrifuges. High-speed centrifuges operate at greater rotational speeds, typically exceeding 10,000 RPM, making them suitable for applications requiring the rapid separation of biological samples, such as cell cultures and proteins. Low-speed centrifuges, operating below 3,000 RPM, are ideal for processes like cell pelleting and sample preparation where gentle separation is crucial. Each type serves distinct laboratory needs based on the sample and separation requirements.</p></p>
